Weight vs. Hemoglobin A1C An Adventure in Scrollytelling

Scrollytelling is a neologism of scroll and storytelling. The concept is to tell a story with data as a user scrolls through a web site. Our picture starts simple, and we add more and more elements as the user reads through the data. We make a heavy use of transitions, so changes are not jarring to the user.

Introducing the Subjects

Let's start by generating 200 sample subjects. This data has been randomly generated using an Excel spreadsheet.

By Sex

Let's apply colors so we can distinguish between female and male subjects.

By Weight

Next, we apply an x-axis to show our data points by weight (in pounds). Note how the points keep their y-position while moving to their appropriate x coordinate in the visualization.

By A1C

We add hemoglobin A1C, HbA1C (%), to the graph to show the relationship between body weight and A1C.

Subjects with Diabetes

An A1C over 7% indicates that a subject may be a diabetic. Let's filter our graph to only show these data points. Note how the y-axis changes and the points below 7% fall off the bottom of the graph.
