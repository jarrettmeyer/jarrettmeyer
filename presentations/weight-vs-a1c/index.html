Weight vs. Hemoglobin A1C An Adventure in Scrollytelling

Note: All data for this presentation was randomly generated. Nothing is real. Nothing can be trusted.

Scrollytelling is a neologism of scroll and storytelling. It is the marriage of writing, data, and time, brought to life by the web user. Our picture starts simple, and we add more and more elements as the user reads through the data. Additionally, we make a heavy use of transitions, so changes are not jarring to the user.

Since scrollytelling is meant to tell a coherent story with your data, expect to have greater requirements from your writing teams and partners.

A subject is considered diabetic if Hemoglobin A1C is greater than 7.0%. Let's drop all points with HbA1C < 7%. Notice how the scale slides and points drop off the bottom of the visualization.

Weight Loss vs. Change in A1C

For subjects who are diabetic, losing weight may help reduce A1C. This shows the results of subjects who succeeded at following an exercise regimen over six (6) months. A green circle around a point indicates a patient who transitioned to a healthy A1C (i.e. < 7%).
