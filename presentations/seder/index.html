A Journey Through the Jewish Seder

Jarrett Meyer — 18 May 2019

History

  • 15c BCE: Israelites are liberated from Egypt.
  • 14c BCE: Joshua, successor to Moses, celebrates the Passover at Gilgal, near Jericho.

For 300 years, the Passover is not celebrated. Only the negative commandment (do not consume leavened bread) is observed.

  • 11c BCE: Samuel institutes the practice of Passover.
  • 10c BCE: Solomon commissions the construction of the First Temple.

History

  • 5c BCE: The Torah is written. For the last 1000 years, Torah has been an oral tradition.
  • 587 BCE: Nebuchanezzar II of Babylon sieges Jerusalem and destroys the First Temple.
  • 515 BCE: Construction of the Second Temple begins.

Passover During the Second Temple

Animals would be purchased or brought to the Temple for slaughter. When the masses were gathered in the Temple court, they would bar the Temple gates and blow the shofar.

The kohanim would sacrifice the animals, catching their blood in golden bowls. The levites would carry the blood to the altar and pour out the blood.

The levites would join with the gathered masses to sing Hallel (praise) Psalms.

Passover During the Second Temple

Meat would be charred and passed around. Bitter herbs, maror, would be consumed.

Children were encouraged to learn about the story of Exodus.

At some point in time, charoset was introduced.

Passover During the Second Temple

The Passover, Pesach, is a single day Temple ritual, requiring priests, animal sacrifice, and a blood ritual.

The Feast of Unleavened Bread, Chag Hamatzot, is a seven day practice, forbidding the consumption of chametz.

Jesus of Nazareth would not have celebrated a home Passover meal. (It did not exist yet!)

History

  • 37 BCE: The reign of King Herod the Great begins.
  • 20 BCE: Herod greatly expands the role of the Second Temple (sometime's referred to as Herod's Temple). The Temple employs over 1,000 priests.
  • 4 BCE: King Herod the Great dies. He leaves his kingdom to his four children.
    • Herod Archaleus: Judea, Samaria, Idumea.
    • Herod Antipas: Galilee, Perea.
    • Philip: Batanea.
    • Salome I: Decapolis, Yavne.

History

  • 26 CE: Pontius Pilate becomes the 5th Roman Prefect of Judea.
  • 30 CE: Jesus of Nazareth is crucified.
  • 66 CE: Zealots claim Jerusalem from the Romans.
  • 70 CE: Titus sieges Jerusalem and destroys the Second Temple.

History

  • 66 - 70 CE: Mark is written.
  • 75 - 94 CE: Josephus Flavius writes Antiquities of the Jews.
  • 90 - 110 CE: Luke-Acts is written.
  • ~200 CE: The Mishnah (oral tradition) is written.
  • ~500 CE: The Gemara (halacha and theology) is written.
